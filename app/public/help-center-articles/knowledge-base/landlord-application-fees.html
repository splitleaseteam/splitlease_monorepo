How Much Can Landlords Charge for Application Fees?

It depends‚Äîsome states cap application fees, while others allow landlords to charge what they like.

Landlords will typically ask for a small payment‚Äîknown as an "application fee"‚Äîwhen you apply to rent one of their units. This fee is supposed to pay for any background or credit checks that a landlord runs as part of their approval process. It's not refundable.

State-Specific Regulations

Certain states, including New York and California, have a hard cap on how much a landlord can charge you for an application fee. Other states don't explicitly mention a dollar amount, but they do say that the application fee can't exceed the landlord's actual costs in conducting any background checks.

States With Caps

In states with caps, landlords are legally limited in how much they can charge. Always check your state's specific regulations before paying an application fee.

States Without Explicit Limits

There are also states, such as Texas or New Jersey, that don't limit application fees at all. Although that means a landlord can legally charge as much as they want, most online services for credit and background checks cost between $30-40.

Assuming a landlord also factors in their time spent checking in with previous landlords, you shouldn't be seeing application fees much higher than $50 or $60 at the very most.

Watch Out for Excessive Fees

If you're being asked to pay a fee that's way higher, consider looking elsewhere for an apartment‚Äîlandlords shouldn't be using application fees as a way to make money.

What Application Fees Should Cover

Application fees are intended to cover:

  • Credit check costs - Typically $20-30
  • Background check costs - Usually $10-20
  • Verification calls - Time spent calling previous landlords
  • Administrative processing - Reviewing application documents

Reasonable Fee Ranges

Type Typical Cost Notes
Credit Check $20-$30 Most services charge this range
Background Check $10-$20 Criminal history screening
Administrative Time $10-$20 Processing and verification
Total Reasonable Fee $40-$60 Maximum you should expect

Your Rights as a Renter

  • Check state laws: Know your state's specific caps and regulations
  • Ask for itemization: Request a breakdown of what the fee covers
  • Get receipts: Always get documentation of payment
  • Know it's non-refundable: Application fees are typically not returned
  • Report excessive fees: Contact local housing authorities if fees seem unreasonable
