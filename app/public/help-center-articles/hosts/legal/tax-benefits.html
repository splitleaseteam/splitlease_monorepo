What tax benefits can I get as a host?

Split Lease hosting offers significant tax benefits. Many hosts deduct $5,000-15,000+ annually, substantially reducing their tax burden.

Tax Disclaimer

This is educational information only. Consult a CPA or tax professional for advice specific to your situation.

Major Tax Deductions

1. Mortgage Interest

  • Deduct: Portion of mortgage interest based on rental use
  • Example: Guest uses 40% of space, deduct 40% of interest
  • Typical savings: $2,000-6,000/year
  • Form: Schedule E (not Schedule A)

2. Property Taxes

  • Deduct: Proportionate share of property taxes
  • Example: $6,000 annual taxes √ó 40% rental use = $2,400 deduction
  • Fully deductible: Unlike personal property tax limits

3. Depreciation

  • Deduct: Property value (not land) over 27.5 years
  • Example: $300,000 building value √∑ 27.5 = $10,909/year √ó 40% use = $4,364 deduction
  • Bonus: Depreciate furniture, appliances separately (5-7 years)
  • No cash outlay: Paper deduction reducing taxes without spending money

4. Utilities

  • Electric
  • Gas
  • Water/sewer
  • Trash
  • Internet/cable
  • Phone (business line)

Deduct: Proportionate share based on rental use

5. Repairs and Maintenance

  • Fully deductible in year incurred: Paint, fix leaks, repair appliances
  • Cleaning services: Between guest stays
  • Landscaping/snow removal
  • Pest control
  • HVAC maintenance

6. Improvements vs. Repairs

Repairs: Deduct immediately (fix broken toilet)

Improvements: Depreciate over time (new kitchen)

  • New roof: Depreciate
  • Fix leak: Deduct immediately
  • New appliances: Depreciate (5 years)
  • Repair appliance: Deduct immediately

7. Insurance

  • Landlord/rental insurance
  • Liability insurance
  • Flood insurance
  • Umbrella policy (proportionate)

8. HOA/Condo Fees

  • Proportionate share deductible
  • Special assessments (may depreciate if improvement)

9. Management and Admin

  • Split Lease service fees (10-15% fully deductible)
  • Accounting/bookkeeping services
  • Legal fees
  • Property management fees
  • Advertising costs

10. Supplies

  • Cleaning supplies
  • Linens and towels
  • Toilet paper, soap, etc.
  • Light bulbs
  • Small tools
  • Office supplies

11. Travel

  • Mileage: $0.67/mile (2024 rate) for property visits
  • Parking and tolls
  • If property far from home: Travel costs to manage property

12. Professional Services

  • CPA/tax preparation
  • Attorney fees
  • Home inspection
  • Appraisal
  • Consulting

13. Home Office

  • If you qualify: Exclusive regular use for rental management
  • Deduct: Portion of home expenses (mortgage interest, taxes, utilities, depreciation)
  • Simplified option: $5/sq ft up to 300 sq ft ($1,500 max)

Calculating Your Deductions

Proportionate Use Method

Two factors:

  • Space: Rental room as % of total home
  • Time: Days rented √∑ 365

Example:

  • 1 bedroom = 25% of home
  • Rented 120 days/year = 33% of time
  • 25% √ó 33% = 8.25% for shared expenses
  • Direct rental expenses = 100% deductible

Income Reporting

How Income is Taxed

  • Rental income is ordinary income (not capital gains)
  • Taxed at your marginal rate
  • Report on Schedule E
  • Deductions reduce taxable income

What Split Lease Provides

  • 1099-K if you earn $600+/year
  • Annual earnings summary
  • Transaction history
  • Easy export for tax software

Quarterly Estimated Taxes

When Required

If you expect to owe $1,000+ in taxes, make quarterly payments:

  • April 15
  • June 15
  • September 15
  • January 15 (next year)

How to Calculate

  • Estimate annual rental income
  • Subtract estimated deductions
  • Calculate tax on net income
  • Divide by 4 for quarterly amount

Special Situations

Rent Your Primary Residence

  • Can still deduct proportionate expenses
  • Capital gains exclusion ($250k/$500k) still applies when you sell
  • Track rental vs. personal use carefully

Short-term vs. Long-term Rental

  • Split Lease periodic tenancy: Treated as long-term rental for tax purposes
  • Benefit: Avoid some short-term rental restrictions

Active vs. Passive Income

  • Rental income generally passive
  • $25,000 passive loss allowance if you actively participate
  • Phase-out at $100k-150k AGI

Record Keeping

What to Track

  • Income: Every payment received
  • Expenses: Every deductible expense with receipts
  • Mileage: Date, destination, miles, purpose
  • Asset purchases: Date, cost, useful life
  • Rental days: Calendar of guest stays

How Long to Keep

  • Tax returns: Forever
  • Supporting documents: 7 years
  • Property records: 7 years after sale

Tools

  • Accounting software (QuickBooks, Wave)
  • Expense tracking apps
  • Digital receipt storage
  • Mileage tracking apps

Working with a CPA

When to Hire

  • First year hosting (set up properly)
  • Multiple properties
  • Significant income ($20k+/year)
  • Complex situation (LLC, S-corp, etc.)
  • Audit concerns

What They Do

  • Maximize deductions
  • Ensure compliance
  • Handle quarterly payments
  • Tax planning strategies
  • Audit support

Cost vs. Benefit

  • CPA fees: $500-2,000/year (deductible!)
  • Typical additional tax savings: $2,000-5,000+
  • ROI: Usually 2-5x fee paid

Tax Planning Strategies

Maximize Deductions

  • Make repairs in rental years (not vacant years)
  • Bunch expenses when income is high
  • Accelerate depreciation on assets
  • Consider cost segregation study (larger properties)

Timing

  • Pay January expenses in December for current year deduction
  • Defer income to next year if beneficial
  • Time major purchases strategically

Summary

Tax Benefits Recap

  • Deduct mortgage interest, property taxes, depreciation
  • Repairs, utilities, insurance fully deductible
  • Split Lease fees are deductible
  • Typical deductions: $5,000-15,000+/year
  • Keep detailed records of everything
  • Consult a CPA for personalized strategy
  • Tax benefits often equal 20-30% of gross rental income

Remember: Tax benefits can significantly increase your net hosting income. Work with a professional to maximize savings legally.
