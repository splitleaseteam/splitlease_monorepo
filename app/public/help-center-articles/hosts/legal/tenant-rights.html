I am worried about my guest gaining tenant's rights. What are my protections?

We understand this is a major concern for property owners. Let's address the key differences between short-term rentals and 30+ day rentals, and explain the multiple layers of protection Split Lease provides.

Understanding the Landscape

A major difference between short-term rentals and 30+ day rentals is that tenants do gain certain rights. Depending on your state or municipality and the current political climate, you may have an easier or tougher time getting an unpaying tenant to leave.

This is why Split Lease's protections are so important

We've designed our entire system - from the rental patterns we encourage to our legal agreements - to minimize these risks for hosts.

Protection #1: Split Schedule Patterns

At Split Lease, we encourage rentals for weekdays separately from weekends. This pattern of behavior significantly reduces the chance that you would get a squatter when the tenant becomes a medium-term renter.

Why this works:

  • Most work-week home travelers are traveling for business
  • They already have a primary address that they return to each week
  • Your property serves as their secondary address, not their primary residence
  • This pattern makes it legally clear the space is not their primary dwelling

Protection #2: Explicit Roommate Agreement Terms

Section 6 of our Roommate Agreement requires subtenants to agree explicitly to the following:

  1. That the subleased property is not intended to become their Primary Residence
  2. That this Roommate Agreement is specifically exempted from landlord-tenant laws of any kind protecting people living in dwellings owned or leased by others
  3. To forgo any rights of a person staying at the home of another person on a long-term basis (formal or informal)
  4. To vacate the subleased property immediately after the end of the term of this Roommate Agreement

Protection #3: Rewards as Collateral

We can hold any rewards accumulated in the account as collateral on a late check-out. This financial incentive encourages on-time departures and compliance with agreement terms.

Protection #4: Credit Card on File

If the procedures above are not enough, our staff can charge the credit card on file with a late checkout fee. This provides an additional financial deterrent and compensation mechanism.

Best Practices for Hosts

To maximize your protection, we recommend:

  • Screen guests carefully - Use our verification tools and review profiles thoroughly
  • Prefer split schedules - Weekday-only or partial-week rentals carry lower risk
  • Target business travelers - They have established primary residences elsewhere
  • Maintain documentation - Keep all communications and agreements on file
  • Use the platform - Always process payments and communications through Split Lease
