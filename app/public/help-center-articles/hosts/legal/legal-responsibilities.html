What are my legal responsibilities as a host?

As a Split Lease host, you have several legal responsibilities. Understanding these helps protect you and your guests while ensuring compliance.

Legal Disclaimer

This is general guidance only. Consult a local attorney for specific legal advice. Laws vary by state, city, and property type.

Core Legal Responsibilities

1. Habitability Standards

  • Safe living conditions: Property must be structurally sound
  • Working utilities: Heat, water, electricity functional
  • Clean and sanitary: No pest infestations, mold, hazards
  • Security: Working locks on doors and windows
  • Building codes: Meet local housing standards

2. Property Disclosures

Must disclose:

  • Known defects or issues (leaky roof, faulty wiring)
  • Pest problems
  • Noise issues (nearby construction, loud neighbors)
  • Lead paint (for properties built before 1978)
  • Shared spaces and access arrangements
  • Security concerns or past incidents

3. Tenant Rights Compliance

  • Privacy: Provide notice before entering (usually 24-48 hours)
  • Quiet enjoyment: Don't harass or disturb guests
  • Non-discrimination: Follow Fair Housing laws
  • Retaliation: Can't punish guests for complaints
  • Security deposit: Follow state laws on holding and returning

Split Lease Specific Considerations

Periodic Tenancy Structure

Split Lease uses periodic tenancy agreements, which means:

  • Guest has legal tenancy rights during booked periods
  • Landlord-tenant laws apply
  • Eviction procedures required if guest won't leave
  • Can't lock out or remove belongings without legal process
  • Must follow proper notice requirements

Storage Responsibilities

If guest stores belongings:

  • Provide secure, dry storage space
  • Don't move or access items without permission
  • Reasonable care to prevent damage
  • Include liability waiver in agreement
  • Insurance considerations (check your policy)

Insurance Requirements

What You Need

  • Landlord insurance: Covers rental activity (not standard homeowner's)
  • Liability coverage: Protects if guest injured on property
  • Property damage: Covers guest-caused damage beyond deposit
  • Loss of income: Optional coverage if property becomes uninhabitable

Notify Your Insurer

Important: Tell insurance company you're renting on Split Lease. Standard homeowner's policies may not cover rental activity.

Tax Obligations

Income Reporting

  • Report all rental income to IRS
  • Split Lease provides 1099 if you earn $600+/year
  • Track expenses for deductions
  • Pay quarterly estimated taxes if significant income
  • State and local income taxes may apply

Occupancy Taxes

  • Some cities charge hotel/occupancy taxes on short-term rentals
  • Usually applies to stays under 30 days
  • Split Lease may collect automatically in some jurisdictions
  • Check with local tax authority

Fair Housing Compliance

Protected Classes

Cannot discriminate based on:

  • Race or color
  • National origin
  • Religion
  • Sex (including gender identity, sexual orientation)
  • Familial status
  • Disability
  • Age (in some states)

What This Means

  • Accept/reject guests based on objective criteria only
  • Can screen for: rental history, credit, income, reviews
  • Can't screen for: personal characteristics, demographics
  • Same rules and pricing for everyone
  • Reasonable accommodations for disabilities

Lease Agreement Requirements

Must Include

  • Names of all parties
  • Property address
  • Rental amount and payment terms
  • Security deposit amount
  • Duration and schedule
  • House rules
  • Termination procedures

Split Lease Provides

  • Standard rental agreement template
  • Covers periodic tenancy structure
  • Compliant with most state laws
  • Customizable for your specific needs

Learn more about lease agreements

Maintenance Obligations

Your Responsibilities

  • Emergency repairs: Heat, water, security (fix immediately)
  • Major repairs: Roof, plumbing, electrical (reasonable timeframe)
  • Appliances: Fix if provided and broken
  • Code violations: Address building code issues

Guest's Responsibilities

  • Minor maintenance (replace light bulbs, batteries)
  • Cleanliness and sanitation
  • Report problems promptly
  • Not cause damage through negligence

Safety and Security

Required

  • Smoke detectors: Working, in bedrooms and common areas
  • Carbon monoxide detectors: If gas/fuel-burning appliances
  • Fire extinguisher: Accessible location
  • Emergency exits: Clear and marked
  • Secure locks: Deadbolts on exterior doors

Recommended

  • First aid kit
  • Emergency contact list
  • Flashlights with batteries
  • Clear exit plan

Record Keeping

What to Keep

  • Rental agreements (7 years)
  • Payment records (7 years)
  • Move-in/move-out inspections
  • Communication with guests
  • Repair and maintenance records
  • Tax documents
  • Insurance policies

Local Regulations

Check Requirements

May need:

  • Business license
  • Short-term rental permit (usually not for periodic tenancy)
  • HOA approval
  • Landlord permission (if you're a renter)
  • Zoning compliance

Research Your Area

  • City/county regulations
  • HOA/condo rules
  • Lease restrictions
  • Occupancy limits

Risk Management

Best Practices

  • Screen guests thoroughly
  • Use written agreements always
  • Document everything (photos, emails)
  • Respond to issues promptly
  • Maintain property properly
  • Keep detailed financial records
  • Consult professionals when needed

Summary

Key Legal Responsibilities

  • Provide safe, habitable property
  • Disclose known issues
  • Follow Fair Housing laws
  • Respect tenant rights
  • Maintain proper insurance
  • Report income and pay taxes
  • Follow legal eviction procedures
  • Keep detailed records

When in doubt, consult an attorney. Better to get professional advice than face legal issues later.
