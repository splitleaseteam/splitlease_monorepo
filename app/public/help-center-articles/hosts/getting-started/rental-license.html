Can I list without a short-term rental license?

Great question! In most cases, yes - you can list on Split Lease without a short-term rental license. Here's why: Split Lease operates under periodic tenancy (long-term rental) laws, not short-term rental regulations.

Good News for Most Hosts

Split Lease's periodic tenancy model typically does not require short-term rental licenses that platforms like Airbnb need.

Why Split Lease is Different

Periodic Tenancy vs. Short-Term Rentals

Feature Split Lease Airbnb/VRBO
Rental Type Periodic tenancy Short-term rental
Guest Status Tenant Guest
Minimum Stay 30+ nights/month 1+ nights
License Required Usually no Usually yes

Legal Classification

Split Lease guests are tenants, not transient guests:

  • Month-to-month periodic tenancy agreement
  • 30+ day booking commitments
  • Tenant rights and protections
  • Subject to landlord-tenant law, not hotel/STR regulations

When You DON'T Need a License

Most Split Lease hosts are exempt because:

  • 30+ day stays: Exceed STR minimum thresholds
  • Periodic tenancy: Classified as traditional rental
  • Single tenant: Not nightly turnover like hotels
  • Residential use: Guest lives there part-time, not vacation

When You MIGHT Need Permits

Check Local Laws If:

  • Your building prohibits rentals: HOA or condo rules
  • Rent control areas: Some jurisdictions restrict subletting
  • Zoning restrictions: Some residential zones prohibit all rentals
  • Business license: Some cities require general business license for rental income

Always Verify Locally

While Split Lease's model typically avoids STR license requirements, local laws vary. Check your city/county regulations or consult a local attorney.

City-Specific Examples

New York City

  • STR law: Cannot rent entire apartment < 30 days
  • Split Lease: 30+ day periodic tenancy = compliant
  • License needed: No (for periodic tenancy)

San Francisco

  • STR law: Must register and get license
  • Split Lease: 30+ days = long-term rental, not STR
  • License needed: No (periodic tenancy exempt)

Los Angeles

  • STR law: Registration required for < 30 days
  • Split Lease: Month-to-month tenancy
  • License needed: No

What You Should Do

Step 1: Understand Your Classification

You're offering:

  • Long-term periodic tenancy
  • Partial-use residential rental
  • Not transient lodging or short-term rental

Step 2: Check These Sources

  • City website: Search "[Your City] rental regulations"
  • Landlord-tenant laws: Your state's residential rental rules
  • Building/HOA rules: Review your lease or bylaws
  • Attorney: 30-minute consultation for peace of mind

Step 3: Document Your Compliance

  • Keep copy of lease/tenancy agreement
  • Document 30+ day booking periods
  • Show it's periodic tenancy, not nightly rental
  • Maintain records for tax purposes

Common Concerns Addressed

"My city has strict STR laws"

Response:

Good! Split Lease was designed specifically for cities with strict STR regulations. Periodic tenancy isn't subject to those laws.

"My HOA prohibits Airbnb"

Response:

Most HOA rules targeting "short-term rentals" define them as < 30 days. Periodic tenancy (30+ days) is typically allowed. Review your HOA documents or ask management.

"I'm worried about legal issues"

Response:

Split Lease provides legally-compliant periodic tenancy agreements. You're operating as a traditional landlord, just with flexible scheduling. Consult local attorney if concerned.

Tax Implications

Benefits of periodic tenancy:

Insurance Considerations

What You Need

  • Landlord insurance: Standard policy for rental properties
  • NOT STR insurance: You're not doing short-term rentals
  • Liability coverage: Protects you as property owner
  • Inform your insurer: Tell them you're renting to long-term tenant
