What is Periodic Tenancy?

Periodic tenancy is the legal framework that makes Split Lease different from hotels and Airbnb. Understanding it helps you know your rights and why Split Lease offers better value and flexibility.

Simple Definition

Periodic tenancy means you're a tenant with a month-to-month lease, not a hotel guest. You get more rights, better pricing, and flexibility - without being locked into a long-term lease.

Benefits of Periodic Tenancy

1. Better Pricing Structure

No surprise fees:

  • No cleaning fee per booking
  • No service fees on every payment
  • No resort fees or surprise charges
  • Simple monthly rate

Volume discount pricing:

  • Monthly commitment = lower per-night cost
  • Typically 30-50% cheaper than Airbnb
  • But 30-50% cheaper than full-time apartment
  • You only pay for nights you actually use

2. Legal Protections as Tenant

You have rights:

  • Can't be removed without proper notice
  • Damage deposit held in escrow
  • Privacy rights (host must give notice before entry)
  • Protection under rental laws

Your Tenant Rights

As a periodic tenant, you have the same legal protections as any other tenant - but with month-to-month flexibility instead of being locked into a year-long lease.

3. Flexibility to Change

Month-to-month means:

  • Give 30 days notice to end tenancy
  • No penalty for leaving
  • No long-term commitment required
  • Adjust your schedule as needed

Unlike fixed-term leases:

  • No 6-12 month obligation
  • No breaking lease penalties
  • No hunting for sublet
  • No first/last month deposit

4. Storage Rights

Because you're a tenant:

  • Legal right to store belongings
  • Host cannot remove your items
  • Your space is your space
  • Keep work equipment, clothes, toiletries permanently

5. Stability and Consistency

Not just a guest:

  • Build relationship with host
  • Reliable access to same space
  • Not competing with other bookings
  • Your "second home" is yours

How Periodic Tenancy Works in Practice

The Agreement

When you book:

  • Sign a periodic tenancy agreement (digital)
  • Specifies your rental schedule (e.g., Mon-Wed each week)
  • Monthly rate for your nights
  • Auto-renews each month unless you give notice

Payment Structure

Typical payment:

  • First month due at booking
  • Then auto-charged on the 1st of each month
  • Damage deposit (if applicable)
  • No hidden fees or surprise charges

Example Pricing:

You stay Monday-Wednesday (12 nights/month) at $90/night = $1,080/month.

Compared to Airbnb at $140/night + fees = $1,968/month for same nights.

Periodic tenancy saves you $888/month!

Modifying Your Schedule

Need to change nights?

  • Coordinate with host
  • More flexible than you'd think
  • Most hosts accommodate reasonable changes
  • Agreement can be amended

Ending the Tenancy

When you're ready to leave:

  • Give 30 days written notice
  • Complete final month
  • Do final walk-through
  • Get damage deposit back (minus any legitimate deductions)
  • Leave review

Common Questions About Periodic Tenancy

Do I need a lease agreement?

Yes, but it's simple and handled digitally through Split Lease. Takes 5 minutes to review and sign.

What if I want to leave early?

Give 30 days notice anytime. You're not locked in. There's no penalty, just fulfill your notice period.

Can the host kick me out?

No, not without proper cause and notice. You have tenant protections. Host can't just decide to end your tenancy on a whim.

What if the host sells the property?

Your tenancy transfers to the new owner. You must be given proper notice if they want to terminate.

Am I considered a long-term tenant?

Legally yes, even though you don't use the space full-time. You're a tenant with partial use rights on specific days.

Do I pay utilities?

Usually included in your rent. Specifics are in your rental agreement. Most hosts include all utilities for simplicity.

Can I have guests?

Depends on your agreement and house rules. Many hosts allow occasional guests. Discuss upfront.

What about mail delivery?

As a tenant, you can receive mail there. Check with your host about logistics.

Why Split Lease Uses Periodic Tenancy

Perfect for Multi-Local Living

Periodic tenancy solves the core problem for multi-locals:

  • Too flexible for traditional lease: Don't need full-time space
  • Too expensive for Airbnb: Nightly fees add up fast
  • Too unstable for hotels: Need same place, not random rooms

Periodic tenancy is the goldilocks solution - just right for people who live in multiple places.

Tax Advantages for Hosts

Periodic tenancy vs. short-term rentals:

  • Different tax treatment
  • Can qualify as passive income
  • No short-term rental licenses in many cities
  • Hosts save money, pass savings to you

Better Host-Guest Relationships

Periodic tenancy encourages:

  • Long-term partnerships
  • Mutual respect and trust
  • Cooperation and flexibility
  • Better experiences for everyone
