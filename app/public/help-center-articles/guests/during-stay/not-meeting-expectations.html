What if the place doesn't meet my expectations?

Not every space will be perfect, and that's okay. Split Lease provides multiple solutions depending on the severity of the issue and when you discover it. Here's your complete guide to resolving problems.

You're Protected

Between the money-back guarantee, flexible cancellation, and platform support, you have multiple options to resolve issues. You're never stuck.

Step 1: Identify the Issue Type

Major Problems (Immediate Action)

Serious issues requiring quick resolution:

  • Safety hazards (broken locks, electrical issues, gas leaks)
  • Unlivable conditions (no heat, no water, severe uncleanliness)
  • Major misrepresentation (wrong location, fake photos, different property)
  • Host harassment or inappropriate behavior

Action: Contact Split Lease support immediately + invoke money-back guarantee

Moderate Problems (Fixable)

Issues that can be resolved:

  • Broken appliances or amenities
  • Cleanliness below standards
  • Missing items from listing
  • WiFi too slow for work
  • Noise issues

Action: Communicate with host + request fixes

Minor Annoyances (Adapt or Leave)

Subjective preferences:

  • Decor not your style
  • Mattress firmness
  • Neighborhood vibe
  • Commute longer than expected
  • Space feels smaller than imagined

Action: Decide if you can adapt or give 30-day notice

Solutions Based on Timing

Within First 48 Hours: Money-Back Guarantee

If you discover major issues immediately:

  1. Document problems with photos/videos
  2. Contact Split Lease support via app or email
  3. Explain issue clearly with evidence
  4. Request full refund under guarantee
  5. Platform reviews claim (usually within 12-24 hours)

Eligible issues: Major discrepancies, safety problems, uninhabitable conditions

Not eligible: Subjective preferences, minor issues, changed your mind

Learn more about the money-back guarantee

Within First Month: Trial Period Mindset

Give it a fair shot:

  • Some things take getting used to
  • First impressions aren't always accurate
  • Neighborhood might grow on you
  • Work with host to fix specific issues

But if it's not working:

  • Give 30-day notice anytime
  • Find alternative in parallel
  • Book trial night elsewhere
  • Make clean transition

After Several Months: Relationship Approach

You've built a relationship with host:

  • Discuss issues openly
  • Host likely wants to keep you happy
  • Request improvements or adjustments
  • Negotiate better terms if needed
  • Last resort: 30-day notice

Communication Strategy

Good communication template:

"Hi [Host], I wanted to bring up [specific issue]. I've noticed [describe problem objectively]. Would it be possible to [proposed solution]? I'm otherwise enjoying the space and would love to work this out. Thanks!"

Document Everything

Keep records of:

  • All communications with host
  • Photos/videos of issues
  • Timestamps showing when problems arose
  • Any promises host made
  • Screenshots of original listing

Resolution Paths

Path 1: Host Fixes the Problem

Best case scenario:

  • Host acknowledges issue
  • Takes action to resolve
  • Problem gets fixed
  • You continue happily
  • Mention in review that host was responsive

Path 2: Negotiate Compensation

If problem can't be fully fixed:

  • Request rent reduction
  • Get credit for future stays
  • Waive certain fees
  • Host covers cost of workaround

Example: WiFi too slow for work ‚Üí Host pays for coworking space day pass

Path 3: Mutual Agreement to End

Sometimes it's just not a fit:

  • Both parties acknowledge mismatch
  • Agree to end early (waive 30-day notice)
  • Part on good terms
  • Host refunds prorated amount
  • Both leave fair reviews

Path 4: Platform Mediation

If you can't resolve directly:

  • Contact Split Lease support
  • Explain situation with evidence
  • Platform reviews both sides
  • Makes fair determination
  • May offer relocation assistance

Path 5: Standard Cancellation

Always your right:

  • Give 30 days written notice
  • No justification required
  • Find new place during notice period
  • Move on cleanly

Common Issues & Solutions

Issue: WiFi Too Slow

Solutions:

  • Ask host to upgrade internet plan
  • Use ethernet cable instead of WiFi
  • Work from nearby coworking space some days
  • Use mobile hotspot for critical work
  • Request rent reduction if unfixable

Issue: Noise Problems

Solutions:

  • Invest in quality earplugs or white noise machine
  • Ask host about soundproofing improvements
  • Adjust your schedule (sleep earlier/later)
  • Request room with less noise exposure
  • If severe and undisclosed: money-back guarantee

Issue: Cleanliness Standards

Solutions:

  • Communicate specific concerns
  • Request better cleaning between your stays
  • Offer to self-clean for reduced rate
  • Do light cleaning yourself
  • If egregious: report to platform

Issue: Broken Amenities

Solutions:

  • Report immediately
  • Give host reasonable time to repair (3-7 days)
  • Request temp workaround
  • If critical amenity: request alternative accommodation
  • Rent reduction if can't be fixed quickly

Issue: Different Than Photos

Solutions:

  • If minor: decide if you can live with it
  • If significant: money-back guarantee (within 48 hours)
  • Request rent reduction for ongoing difference
  • After 48 hours: give notice and find better match

Preventing Disappointment

Before You Book

Do your homework:

  • Always do trial night first - catches 90% of issues
  • Ask detailed questions
  • Request video walkthrough
  • Read all reviews carefully
  • Research neighborhood independently
  • Clarify all assumptions

Manage Expectations

Remember:

  • Photos show best angles
  • Spaces often look bigger in photos
  • Some wear and tear is normal
  • No place is perfect
  • Shared spaces mean compromises

Know Your Deal-Breakers

Before booking, identify:

  • Must-haves (fast WiFi, quiet, good mattress)
  • Nice-to-haves (gym, outdoor space, dishwasher)
  • Deal-breakers (pets, smoking, stairs)

Verify must-haves explicitly. Accept compromises on nice-to-haves. Never compromise on deal-breakers.

Your Rights & Protections

Multi-Layer Protection

  • Money-back guarantee: First 48 hours
  • Platform support: Mediation anytime
  • Flexible cancellation: 30-day notice always available
  • Tenant rights: Legal protections as periodic tenant
  • Review system: Hold hosts accountable

Mindset Shift

It's Not Personal

Not every match works out. That doesn't make you a bad guest or them a bad host. Sometimes it's just not the right fit, and that's okay.

Communication Solves Most Problems

90% of issues get resolved when guests speak up respectfully. Hosts want happy long-term guests. Give them a chance to make it right.

You Have Options

Unlike a traditional 12-month lease, you're not trapped. With 30-day notice, you can always find something better if needed.

Learn and Improve

Each experience teaches you what to look for, what questions to ask, and what matters most to you. Your next booking will be better informed.

When to Leave vs. When to Stay

Strong Signals to Leave

  • Safety concerns
  • Host unresponsive to serious issues
  • Major misrepresentation
  • Your health/wellbeing affected
  • Fundamental incompatibility

Reasons to Try Sticking It Out

  • Minor fixable issues
  • Host is responsive and trying
  • Good location/price
  • Issue might resolve naturally
  • Alternatives aren't clearly better

Summary: Your Action Plan

When something isn't right:

  1. Assess severity: Major problem vs. minor annoyance?
  2. Document: Photos, messages, timestamps
  3. Communicate: Tell host specifically and respectfully
  4. Give reasonable time: Let host attempt to fix
  5. Escalate if needed: Platform support or money-back guarantee
  6. Final option: 30-day notice to leave

Remember

Trial nights exist precisely to catch mismatches before long-term commitment. Use them! And if something still goes wrong, you have multiple paths to resolution. You're never stuck.
