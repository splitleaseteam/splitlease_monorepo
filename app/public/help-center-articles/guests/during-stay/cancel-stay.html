What happens if I want to cancel during my stay?

Life changes happen. Whether it's a job change, relocation, or shift in circumstances, Split Lease's periodic tenancy gives you flexibility to end your arrangement. Here's exactly how cancellation works and what to expect.

Standard Notice Period

Give 30 days written notice to end your tenancy. No penalty, no questions asked - just fulfill your notice period and you're free to go.

How Cancellation Works

Step 1: Give Written Notice

Provide 30 days notice:

  • Message host through platform
  • State your intention to end tenancy
  • Specify your final date (30+ days out)
  • Keep copy of notice for records

Step 2: Complete Notice Period

During your final 30 days:

  • Continue paying rent as scheduled
  • Use your reserved nights
  • Remove personal belongings gradually
  • Coordinate final walkthrough

Step 3: Final Checkout

On your last day:

  • Remove all belongings
  • Clean space (or arrange final cleaning)
  • Return keys/access devices
  • Do walkthrough with host (if possible)
  • Document final condition with photos

Step 4: Get Deposit Back

Within 14-30 days:

  • Host inspects for damage
  • Any legitimate deductions itemized
  • Remaining deposit refunded
  • Both leave reviews

Cancellation Timeline Examples

Example 1: Standard Notice

  • Today (May 1): You give notice
  • May 1-31: You continue normally, pay May rent
  • May 31: Your last scheduled night, final checkout
  • June 15: Deposit refunded
  • Result: Clean break, no penalties

Financial Implications

What You Pay

During notice period:

  • Full rent for the final 30-day period
  • Any scheduled nights you actually use
  • No cancellation fee
  • No penalty charges

What You Get Back

Refunds:

  • Security/damage deposit (minus legitimate deductions)
  • Any prepaid rent beyond notice period
  • Prorated amount if you leave early (at host discretion)

What You Don't Get Back

Non-refundable:

  • Rent already paid for completed months
  • Nights you already stayed
  • Current month's rent (your notice period)

Special Circumstances

Emergency Situations

Job loss, family emergency, health issues:

  • Explain situation to host
  • Many hosts are understanding
  • May waive or reduce notice period
  • Work out arrangement that's fair to both parties
  • Get any agreement in writing

Immediate Cancellation

Need to leave right away:

  • Still legally owe 30 days rent
  • Host may allow negotiation
  • Could forfeit deposit
  • May hurt future bookings (reviews)
  • Better to communicate and find compromise

Communication is Key

Hosts often accommodate emergencies if you're honest and respectful. A guest who communicated openly will get better treatment than one who disappears.

Moving to Different Location

Relocating within Split Lease:

  • Give notice at current location
  • Book new location (mention you're existing user)
  • Some hosts offer transition flexibility
  • Keep good standing for easier approvals

Reducing Financial Impact

Find a Replacement Guest

Help host rebook your nights:

  • Refer colleagues or friends
  • Post in professional networks
  • Help host fill your schedule
  • Host may release you early if replacement found

Negotiate Transition

Offer solutions:

  • "I can give 45 days notice instead of 30"
  • "I'll help you find a new guest"
  • "I'll forfeit deposit instead of 30 days rent"
  • "I'll pay 2 weeks instead of full month"

Reduce Schedule First

Instead of canceling entirely:

  • Reduce from 12 nights/month to 6 nights/month
  • Take a temporary pause (if host agrees)
  • Switch to on-demand instead of scheduled
  • Easier than full cancellation

Your Rights as Tenant

What Host Cannot Do

Host cannot:

  • Keep entire deposit without itemized damages
  • Charge you beyond 30-day notice period
  • Lock you out before notice period ends
  • Keep your belongings
  • Charge "cancellation fees" beyond rent owed

Your Obligations

You must:

  • Give proper written notice (30 days)
  • Pay rent through notice period
  • Return property in good condition
  • Remove all belongings
  • Return access devices (keys, fobs)

Documentation Best Practices

What to Document

Protect yourself:

  • Notice: Screenshot of cancellation message with date
  • Walkthrough: Photos of space in good condition
  • Agreement: Any special arrangements in writing
  • Communications: Save all messages with host
  • Payment records: Receipts through notice period

After Cancellation

Your Split Lease Account

Stays active:

  • Can book with other hosts anytime
  • Reviews and history remain
  • No penalty for future bookings
  • Can return to same host later (if both agree)

Reviews

Both parties review:

  • Leave honest, fair review
  • Acknowledge host's cooperation (if applicable)
  • Don't let cancellation bias your review of the space itself
  • Good exit = good review = easier future bookings

Prevention: Avoiding the Need to Cancel

Start with Trial Nights

  • Test before long-term commitment
  • Verify the fit
  • No long-term obligation if it doesn't work

Commit Conservatively

  • Start with month-to-month
  • Don't prepay many months
  • Keep flexibility until you're certain

Communicate Changes Early

  • Reduction in schedule ‚â† cancellation
  • Adjust before you need to cancel
  • Hosts prefer reduced schedule to losing guest entirely

Sample Cancellation Message

Professional cancellation notice:

"Hi [Host Name],

I wanted to let you know that I need to end my tenancy due to [brief reason - job relocation/change in circumstances]. This serves as my 30-day notice. My final day will be [date 30+ days from today].

I've really enjoyed staying at your place and appreciate how accommodating you've been. I'll make sure to leave everything in great condition and will coordinate the final walkthrough with you.

If I can help find a replacement guest, please let me know. Thank you for everything!

Best regards,
[Your name]"

Summary

Key Takeaways

  • 30 days written notice required
  • No penalty beyond notice period rent
  • Deposit refunded (minus damages)
  • Much more flexible than traditional leases
  • Communicate openly for best outcomes

The beauty of periodic tenancy is the flexibility to leave without being trapped in a long-term commitment. Life changes, and Split Lease adapts with you.
