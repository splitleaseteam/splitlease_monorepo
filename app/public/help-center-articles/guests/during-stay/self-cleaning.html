How can I save money with self cleaning?

Self-cleaning is one of the easiest ways to reduce your monthly Split Lease costs. By maintaining the space yourself instead of paying for cleaning services, you can save $10-25 per night - or $120-300+ per month.

Typical Savings

Self-cleaning guests save $1,500-3,600 per year on cleaning fees while maintaining control over cleanliness standards.

Understanding Cleaning Fees

Typical cleaning fee structures:

  • Per-booking fee: $75-150 per stay (Airbnb style)
  • Per-visit fee: $15-30 per visit (Split Lease)
  • Weekly fee: $50-100/week if you're there multiple days
  • Self-cleaning: $0 - you handle it

What Self-Cleaning Means

Your responsibilities:

  • Make the bed / wash your linens
  • Wipe down bathroom after use
  • Clean kitchen surfaces if you cook
  • Take out trash
  • Vacuum or sweep as needed
  • Keep space tidy

What you DON'T need to do:

  • Deep cleaning (that's still host's responsibility periodically)
  • Window washing
  • Dusting ceiling fans or high surfaces
  • Cleaning areas you don't use

Negotiating Self-Cleaning

When to Bring It Up

Best timing:

  • During initial inquiry: "I'm happy to handle my own cleaning"
  • After trial night: "I keep things very tidy - would you consider waiving cleaning fee?"
  • After a few months: "I've been maintaining the space well - can we adjust the rate?"

How to Propose

Effective scripts:

"I'm very tidy and comfortable handling my own cleaning between visits. Would you be open to removing the cleaning fee in exchange for me maintaining the space? I'll still leave it in great condition."

"To keep costs down, I'd prefer to do my own cleaning. I'll wash my linens, keep the bathroom clean, and maintain everything to your standards. Would that work for you?"

What Hosts Consider

Hosts are more likely to agree if:

  • You're a long-term guest (3+ months)
  • You have positive reviews mentioning cleanliness
  • You've already shown you're responsible (after trial night)
  • You don't use the space heavily (professional use, not cooking every meal)
  • You're willing to do a walk-through inspection periodically

Making Self-Cleaning Easy

Supplies You'll Need

Basic cleaning kit (~$50 one-time):

  • All-purpose cleaner
  • Bathroom cleaner
  • Glass cleaner (if applicable)
  • Microfiber cloths
  • Paper towels
  • Sponges
  • Trash bags
  • Vacuum or broom (often provided)

Many hosts provide cleaning supplies - ask first!

Quick Cleaning Routine

15-minute daily tidying:

  • Morning: Make bed, wipe bathroom sink (2 min)
  • Evening: Wash dishes, wipe kitchen counter (5 min)
  • Before leaving: Take out trash, quick sweep (8 min)

30-minute weekly deep clean:

  • Change linens
  • Clean bathroom thoroughly
  • Vacuum entire space
  • Mop if needed
  • Empty all trash

Time Investment

Most self-cleaning guests spend 15-30 minutes per week maintaining their space. That's a $60-100/hour return on your time!

Benefits Beyond Savings

1. Clean to Your Standards

  • Control exactly how clean it is
  • No waiting for cleaners to come
  • No strangers entering your space
  • Clean on your schedule

2. More Privacy

  • No coordination with cleaning staff
  • No need to clear out for cleaners
  • Your belongings stay undisturbed

3. Better Host Relationship

  • Shows you're responsible
  • Reduces host's costs and hassle
  • Often leads to better rates or renewal terms
  • Builds trust for long-term arrangement

4. Flexibility

  • Stay extra days without coordinating cleaning
  • Change arrival/departure times freely
  • No "cleaning day" restrictions

When Self-Cleaning Might Not Make Sense

Consider professional cleaning if:

  • You're extremely busy with work
  • You value time more than $50/week
  • You have mobility issues
  • The space is very large
  • Host requires professional cleaning
  • You get it very dirty (frequent cooking, pets, etc.)

Hybrid Approach: Best of Both

Many guests do a mix:

  • Weekly self-maintenance: Basic tidying and cleaning
  • Monthly deep clean: Professional cleaner comes once a month
  • Cost: $80/month instead of $240/month
  • Savings: Still $160/month = $1,920/year

Self-Cleaning Agreement Details

What to Document

Put in writing:

  • Cleaning fee removed from monthly rate
  • Your cleaning responsibilities
  • Host's expectations for cleanliness level
  • Periodic inspection schedule (e.g., monthly)
  • What happens if space isn't maintained

Inspection Checkpoints

Host may want to verify quarterly:

  • Overall cleanliness maintained
  • No damage from improper cleaning
  • Appliances kept clean
  • Bathroom properly maintained

This protects both you and the host.

Tips for Success

1. Set Expectations Early

  • Be clear about your cleaning standards
  • Ask host about their expectations
  • Take photos of initial condition
  • Agree on what "clean" means

2. Communicate Issues

  • If something breaks or stains, report it
  • Don't try to hide mistakes
  • Ask how to handle unusual cleaning situations

3. Go Above and Beyond Initially

  • First few cleanings, be extra thorough
  • Prove you take it seriously
  • Builds confidence for long-term arrangement

4. Use Proper Products

  • Don't use harsh chemicals on delicate surfaces
  • Ask host for product recommendations
  • Use microfiber cloths to avoid scratches

Common Self-Cleaning Questions

Q: What if I'm too busy one week?
A: Communicate with host. Most are understanding about occasional lapses if you generally maintain well.

Q: Do I need to clean areas I don't use?
A: Generally no, but wipe them down occasionally so dust doesn't accumulate.

Q: What if I break something while cleaning?
A: Report it immediately. Accidents happen and honesty maintains trust.

Q: Can I get cleaning supplies reimbursed?
A: Some hosts provide them. Ask - worst case, you're spending $50 upfront to save $2,000+/year.

Summary

Self-Cleaning Benefits

  • Save $1,500-3,600 per year
  • Clean to your standards
  • More privacy and flexibility
  • Stronger host relationship
  • Takes only 15-30 min/week

If you're reasonably tidy and don't mind basic cleaning, self-cleaning is one of the smartest ways to reduce your Split Lease costs dramatically.
