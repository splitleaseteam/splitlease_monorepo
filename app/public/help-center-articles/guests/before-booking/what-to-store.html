What things can I store at my Split Lease rental?

Here's a comprehensive breakdown of what you can typically store at your Split Lease rental, organized by category. Remember: always confirm specific storage permissions with your host!

General Guideline

Store items you actively use during your stays. Treat storage as convenience for your regular visits, not as a general storage unit or warehouse.

‚úÖ Almost Always Acceptable

Clothing & Accessories

  • Work clothes: Business casual, suits, dress shirts/blouses
  • Casual outfits: Jeans, t-shirts, loungewear
  • Undergarments & socks: Personal items for your stays
  • Shoes: 2-4 pairs (work shoes, sneakers, casual)
  • Outerwear: Jacket, coat, raincoat (seasonal)
  • Accessories: Belts, ties, scarves, hats
  • Sleepwear & slippers: Comfort items

Toiletries & Personal Care

  • Basic toiletries: Shampoo, conditioner, body wash, toothpaste
  • Skincare: Face wash, moisturizer, sunscreen
  • Hair care: Hair dryer, straightener, styling products
  • Grooming: Razor, nail clippers, tweezers
  • Cosmetics: Makeup, brushes, tools
  • Medications: Personal prescriptions and OTC medicines
  • First aid: Band-aids, pain relievers, basic medical supplies

Work & Tech Equipment

  • Monitor: External display (after host approval)
  • Keyboard & mouse: Ergonomic setup
  • Laptop stand: For better posture
  • Chargers & cables: Phone, laptop, watch, etc.
  • Headphones: For calls and focus
  • Webcam & ring light: For professional video calls
  • Desk lamp: Task lighting
  • Notebooks & pens: Office supplies
  • Portable hard drive: Backups and files

Kitchen & Food Items

  • Non-perishables: Coffee, tea, oatmeal, snacks
  • Condiments: Olive oil, salt, pepper, sauces (that won't spoil)
  • Personal dishware: Favorite mug, water bottle
  • Small appliances: Coffee maker, electric kettle (if not provided)

Perishable Food

Only store perishables in the fridge/freezer, and only what you'll use before your next visit. Remove anything that might spoil if you skip a week.

Bedding & Comfort Items

  • Personal pillow: Your own for better sleep
  • Blanket or throw: Extra comfort layer
  • Sheets (optional): If you prefer your own
  • Towels (optional): Personal bathroom towels

Entertainment & Leisure

  • Books: Current reading material (2-3 books)
  • Magazines & journals: Light reading
  • Streaming device: Roku, Chromecast (if needed)
  • Gaming controller: For entertainment
  • Workout gear: Gym clothes, yoga mat, resistance bands

Bulkier Work Items

  • Printer/scanner: Small, portable model
  • Filing box: For documents and paperwork
  • Whiteboard or corkboard: For planning
  • Multiple monitors: Full dual-screen setup
  • Ergonomic chair: Your own desk chair

Recreational Equipment

  • Musical instruments: Small instruments (guitar, keyboard)
  • Art supplies: Sketchbooks, paint, canvas
  • Sports equipment: Tennis racket, yoga blocks, bike accessories
  • Hobby materials: Knitting supplies, craft tools

Seasonal Items

  • Winter gear: Heavy coat, boots, gloves (seasonal swap)
  • Summer items: Swimwear, sandals, sun hat
  • Umbrella: For rainy weather

Additional Furniture/Decor

  • Small plants: Bring life to the space
  • Photos or artwork: Personal touches (removable hanging)
  • Desk organizers: Make the workspace yours
  • Lamps or lighting: Additional ambiance

Communication is Key

For anything in this category, send a quick message to your host: "Would you mind if I bring X?" Most will say yes, but asking shows respect.

‚ùå Generally Not Acceptable

Prohibited or Problematic Items

  • Hazardous materials: Flammables, chemicals, ammunition
  • Weapons: Guns, knives (beyond kitchen use), pepper spray
  • Illegal substances: Any illegal drugs or paraphernalia
  • Pets/animals: Unless the listing is pet-friendly and agreed upon
  • Large quantities of alcohol: Personal use OK, stockpiling not

Space-Hogging Items

  • Large furniture: Sofas, beds, dressers (unless replacing existing)
  • Big sports equipment: Kayaks, surfboards, skis
  • Extensive collections: 50 books, large media collections
  • Bulky appliances: Space heaters, portable AC, humidifiers
  • Multiple suitcases: Of unused items

Items That Cause Issues

  • Strong-smelling items: Pungent spices, strong incense
  • Items attracting pests: Open food, bird seed
  • Excessive valuables: Large amounts of jewelry, cash, collectibles
  • Someone else's belongings: Don't store items for friends/family

Storage Organization Tips

The Capsule Wardrobe Approach

Most successful Split Lease guests keep a "capsule wardrobe":

  • 5-7 work outfits that mix and match
  • 3-4 casual outfits
  • 2 pairs work shoes, 1 casual, 1 gym/walking
  • 1 week of undergarments and socks
  • 1 workout outfit
  • 1 jacket/coat

This covers all needs without overwhelming storage space.

The Essential Tech Setup

For remote workers:

  • External monitor (24-27")
  • Wireless keyboard and mouse
  • Laptop stand
  • Noise-canceling headphones
  • All chargers and cables
  • Notebook and pens

The Toiletry Basket

Keep everything contained in one or two small bins:

  • Shower/bath products
  • Skincare and cosmetics
  • Grooming tools
  • Hair care items
  • Medications

The "Carry vs. Store" Test

Ask yourself: "Would I want to carry this back and forth every week?" If no, and you use it regularly, it's a good candidate for storage.

Sample Storage Inventory

Minimalist Business Traveler

  • 5 work outfits, 2 casual
  • 2 pairs shoes
  • Basic toiletries
  • Laptop accessories (no monitor)
  • Gym clothes

Storage needed: Half closet, 1 drawer, bathroom shelf

Remote Worker with Full Setup

  • 10 outfits total
  • 3-4 pairs shoes
  • Full toiletry set
  • Monitor, keyboard, mouse, lamp
  • Coffee maker and snacks
  • Workout gear
  • Entertainment (books, streaming device)

Storage needed: Full closet section, 2-3 drawers, desk space, kitchen shelf

Valuable Items & Insurance

For expensive items you're storing (laptop, jewelry, etc.):

  • Understand limits: Hosts aren't responsible for your belongings
  • Get insurance: Renter's insurance often covers items in temporary lodging
  • Use lockbox: For particularly valuable items
  • Don't over-store valuables: Take truly precious items home between stays

Liability Note

Most Split Lease agreements specify that hosts are not liable for stored items. Protect yourself with appropriate insurance if you're storing valuable equipment or belongings.

Seasonal Storage Rotation

Plan to swap items seasonally:

  • Spring/Summer: Replace winter coat with light jacket, bring swimwear
  • Fall/Winter: Swap summer clothes for sweaters and warm layers
  • Quarterly review: Remove items you haven't used in 3 months

This keeps your storage fresh, organized, and respectful of the space.
