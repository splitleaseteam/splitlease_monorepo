How is Split Lease different from Airbnb?

Split Lease offers a fundamentally different rental experience compared to Airbnb, designed specifically for repeat stays and multi-local living. Here are the key differences:

1. Repeat Stays, Not One-Off Bookings

A Split Lease is a repeat stay where you come back to the same place periodically. This creates a true "second home" experience rather than a series of one-time hotel-style stays.

Big Price Discounts

Guests get significant price discounts compared to Airbnb by making a reservation of at least 30 nights. You're rewarded for commitment and consistency.

2. No Hidden Fees

Unlike Airbnb, Split Lease operates under periodic tenancy rather than short-term rentals. This means:

  • No surprise cleaning fees on every booking
  • No excessive service fees
  • Transparent pricing from the start
  • You can even earn back part of the cleaning fee by cleaning up after yourself

3. Store Your Belongings

Since Split Lease is your recurring space, you can store your things at your rental:

  • Leave clothing, toiletries, and personal items
  • Keep a second monitor or work equipment
  • No need to lug suitcases back and forth
  • Treat it like your actual second home

Protected by Guest Guarantee

Your stored belongings are protected under our Guest Guarantee, giving you confidence and peace of mind.

4. Flexibility with Date Changes

Both hosts and guests can easily request amendments to booking dates during the stay. This flexibility is crucial for:

  • Hybrid workers with changing schedules
  • Consultants with shifting project timelines
  • Anyone balancing multiple locations

With Airbnb, changing dates often means canceling and rebooking (with penalties and lost discounts).

5. Rental Type: Periodic Tenancy vs. Short-Term Rental

Split Lease uses periodic tenancy agreements, which means:

  • More stable, long-term arrangements
  • Better legal protections for both parties
  • Lower costs due to reduced transaction overhead
  • Hosts and guests build real relationships
