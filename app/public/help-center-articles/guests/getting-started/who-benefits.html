Who would benefit most from Split Lease?

Split Lease is designed for multi-locals - people who regularly spend time in multiple cities and need a consistent, affordable second home.

Ideal Split Lease Users

1. Corporate Consultants & Business Travelers

If you travel to the same city for work every week or month, Split Lease is perfect for you.

  • Consultants with regular client visits
  • Sales professionals covering specific territories
  • Executives managing teams in multiple locations
  • Professionals attending recurring meetings or training

Real Example

A consultant traveling to NYC every Monday-Thursday saves thousands per month by using Split Lease instead of hotels or Airbnb.

2. Remote Workers & Hybrid Employees

The rise of hybrid work has created a new class of multi-locals:

  • Remote workers who need to be in-office certain days
  • Employees with flexible "work from anywhere" policies
  • Digital nomads who rotate between favorite cities
  • People who want to live near family part-time while working elsewhere

3. Startup Founders & Entrepreneurs

Building a startup often means splitting time between multiple hubs:

  • Founders dividing time between SF, NYC, and other startup hubs
  • Entrepreneurs meeting investors and partners in different cities
  • Business owners managing operations in multiple locations

4. Weekend Commuters

People who work in major metros but want to maintain homes elsewhere:

  • Professionals working weekdays in Manhattan, living elsewhere on weekends
  • People maintaining family homes in suburban/rural areas
  • Those balancing career opportunities with lifestyle preferences

5. Students & Young Professionals

Especially relevant for those in expensive cities:

  • NYU students who go home on weekends
  • Graduate students with research in multiple locations
  • Young professionals exploring different cities
  • Interns and fellows with temporary assignments

Key Characteristics

People who benefit most from Split Lease typically have:

Predictable Patterns
You know roughly when you'll be in each city (every Mon-Thu, every other week, etc.)

Budget Consciousness
You want the consistency of having your own place without paying full-time rent or hotel prices

Light Usage Mindset
You treat the space respectfully as a secondary home, not a party venue

Value Relationships
You prefer building a relationship with one host over constantly booking new places

Budget Range

Most Split Lease users spend $70-120/night depending on location. Since you only pay for nights used, monthly costs are typically 40-60% less than traditional rentals.

Who Split Lease Might NOT Be Right For

To help you make an informed decision, Split Lease may not be ideal if you:

  • Need a place just once or twice (traditional Airbnb is better)
  • Have completely unpredictable travel with no pattern
  • Need full-time primary housing (get a traditional lease)
  • Want to host others or sublet (against most Split Lease agreements)
